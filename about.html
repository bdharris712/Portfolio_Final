5 Things You Won't Think to Ask Me and Which Are Completely Irrelevant Other than to Get to Know Me on a Personal Level

As a "birder," I've been all over the world with my binoculars. I've seen and identified over 190 different species in the wild, including black-mandibled toucans and magnificent frigatebirds in Panama, black swans and purple marsh hens in New Zealand, ostriches and great egrets in Nigeria, vermillion flycatchers, Inca doves, and Zebra doves in Mexico, flamingos in Chile, great sapphirewing hummingbirds in Colombia, red-crowned parrots in California, and tundra swans and burrowing owls in Utah. My trip to Chile failed to find me a Humboldt's penguin so that's still on my bucket list.


I have food-dependent exercise-induced anaphylaxis (FDEIA), which means I am allergic to two of my favorite hobbies: baking and biking. I'm allergic to wheat, but the allergic reaction is only triggered by exercise. I can either eat the doughnuts or I can run off the calories—can't do both.


I am an amateur aviculturalist, and have cared for four species of psittacines (parrots and parakeets). I also have several breeds of chickens, including Silkies, Americaunas, Wyandottes, and ISA Browns. Someday I would like to raise ducks, quail, pheasant, and chukar.


I'm fluent in Spanish and use it regularly, but many people don't know that I also studied Latin, Japanese, and the Elvish langauge Quenya from Lord of the Rings, as well as Palī, the language of the original Buddhist texts. I also enjoy obscure language puns, like,

Q: What is the speed limit in Israel?
A: 五十五 (in Japanese, 55; pronounced 'go juu go')


My wife and I do our own landscaping, and we've cultivated an ecosystem that supports dozens of species of bees and hosts birds through the winter by providing both shelter and food. We have focused mostly on plants native to Utah and other drought-tolerant varieties for a stunning and sustainable landscape. I dug the river-bed by hand—it carries rain water into the yard, ending in a 64 cubic foot dry well, also hand dug.
