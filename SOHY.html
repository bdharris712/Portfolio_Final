Objective

Our task was to redesign the website of Stone of Hope Youth, with particular attention to improving clickthrough to volunteer and donation pages.

Stone of Hope Youth uses a content management system to organize the content of the website, and had a limited understanding of who their audience might be or what users might need from their website.

We decided to retain most of the original content but improve the information architecture and user interface to better meet user needs.

Research and Discovery

Heuristic and competitive analysis in the non-profit and cause-based arena revealed opportunities for immediate improvement. For example, multiple fonts were used where a single choice would offer a less haphazard look. In addition, button colors were not meaningful or purposefully chosen for a particular use case.

Interviews with recent visitors to other non-profit websites revealed further insight into what site visitors aim to do when visiting a new site for the first time. Users want to be able to identify immediately the purpose of the organization and determine its authenticity. In our initial user tests with the client's website, users revealed the site's unclear purpose as a critical problem for us to solve with our redesign project.

Another valuable recommendation from our first round of user tests was the inclusion of a Volunteer tab at the top of the page. Users wanted fast and easy access to information about volunteering, but could not find it where they expected it.

We decided to keep the basic layout of the navigation and the color scheme of the original site, and expanded the use of orange to accent the company's logo.

User Testing Highlight Reel

The New Style

Basing our color palette and typography on existing elements on the website, we created a style guide for the company to use to maintain consistency as they update their CMS pages. We increased white space between page elements to improve legibility at-a-glance for users who are quickly evaluating the legitimacy of the organization.

In addition, we added iconography to the About Us page to highlight the company's core values and tell their story in a more visual way. Testing revealed that this change did help the organization to establish trust with visitors more quickly, and that visitors were more confident about making a donation or exploring the volunteer opportunities.

Some elements on the page were difficult to see, such as navigational affordances on the image carousels. Our new designs incorporated a much more visible user interface using icons that contrasted with the background.

Live Implementation

Within a week of presenting our recommendations to the client, we noticed several major upgrades to Stone of Hope’s live website, including many that we had recommended.

Visitors to the site can now clearly see at a glance what the objectives and mission of the organization are, and are presented with a clear call to action.
