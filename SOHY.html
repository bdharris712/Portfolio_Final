Objective

Our task was to redesign the website of Stone of Hope Youth, with particular attention to improving clickthrough to volunteer and donation pages.

Stone of Hope Youth uses a content management system to organize the content of the website, and had a limited understanding of who their audience might be or what they might need from their website.

For our project, we decided to retain most of the original content and structure of the site, adding and rearranging as needed to better meet user needs.

Research and Discovery

A heuristic analysis and competitive analysis of other non-profit and cause-based websites revealed a few opportunities for immediate improvement. For example, multiple fonts were being used where a single font would have provided a less haphazard look. In addition, the purpose of different button colors was not apparent or meaningful.

Our interviews with recent visitors to various non-profit websites revealed further insight into what site visitors actually aim to do when visiting a new site. One key finding from these interviews is that users want to be able to identify immediately the purpose of the organization. In our initial user tests with the current live version of the website, this was also identified as a critical problem to solve with our redesign project.

Another valuable recommendation from our first round of user tests was the inclusion of a Volunteer tab at the top of the page. Users wanted fast and easy access to information about volunteering, but could not find it where they expected it.

Our team decided to keep the basic layout of the navigation and the color scheme of the original site, and expanded the use of orange to accent the company’s logo.

The New Style

Basing our color palette and typography on elements already existing on the website, we created a style guide for the company to use so that they could maintain consistency as they updated their CMS pages. We increased white space between page elements to improve legibility at-a-glance for users who are quickly evaluating the legitimacy of the organization.

In addition, we added iconography to the About Us page to highlight the company’s core values and tell their story in a more visual way. Testing revealed that this change did help the organization to establish trust with visitors more quickly, and that visitors were more confident about making a donation or exploring the volunteer opportunities as a result of this change.

Some elements on the page were difficult to see, such as navigational affordances on the image carousels. Our new designs incorporated a much more visible user interface using icons that contrasted with the background.

Live Implementation

Within a week of presenting our recommendations to the client, we noticed several major upgrades to Stone of Hope’s live website, including many that we had recommended.

Visitors to the site can now clearly see at a glance what the objectives and mission of the organization are, and are presented with a clear call to action.
